Undergraduate Program

Mechatronics Engineering: Integrated Mechanical, Electronics, Control, and Software

B.Tech in Mechatronics focuses on modeling, sensing, actuation, and intelligent control to build reliable automated systems. Below is a concise overview of the branch.

Program Overview

Mechatronics is an interdisciplinary field combining mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and control engineering. A typical B.Tech program spans 4 years (8 semesters), focusing on modeling, sensing, actuation, control, and intelligent automation.

Key Outcomes

  • Design and integrate mechanical, electronic, and software components.
  • Develop embedded and real‑time control applications.
  • Build and program robots, AGVs, and industrial automation cells.
  • Apply AI/ML for perception and predictive maintenance.
  • Engineer safe, reliable, and maintainable systems.

Who is it for?

Curious builders who enjoy hands‑on making, coding, and systems thinking. If you like robots, drones, cars, or smart devices, this is your track.

Curriculum Snapshot

The exact syllabus varies by university. Below is a representative outline.

Year 1: Foundations
  • Engineering Mathematics I–II (Calculus, Linear Algebra, ODEs)
  • Physics for Engineers; Engineering Chemistry
  • Engineering Graphics and CAD
  • Programming Fundamentals (C/C++/Python)
  • Basic Electrical & Electronics Engineering
  • Workshop Practice and Manufacturing Processes
Year 2: Core Mechatronics
  • Mechanics of Materials; Kinematics & Dynamics of Machines
  • Analog & Digital Electronics; Microcontrollers (8051/AVR/ARM)
  • Signals & Systems; Sensors and Instrumentation
  • Data Structures; Object‑Oriented Programming
  • Thermodynamics and Fluid Mechanics (Applied)
Year 3: Control, Robotics, and Integration
  • Control Systems (Classical and State‑Space)
  • Power Electronics and Drives; Pneumatics & Hydraulics
  • Embedded Systems & RTOS
  • Robot Kinematics, Dynamics, and Programming
  • Computer Vision; Industrial Communication (CAN, Modbus, Ethernet/IP)
Year 4: Advanced Topics and Projects
  • Industrial Automation & PLC/SCADA
  • AI for Mechatronics; Digital Twins & IoT
  • Human‑Robot Interaction; Safety & Standards (ISO 10218, 13849)
  • Electives: Autonomous Vehicles, UAVs, Medical Robotics, Additive Manufacturing
  • Capstone Design Project and Internship

Laboratories & Toolchain

Hardware

  • Oscilloscopes, function generators, power supplies
  • Microcontrollers (ARM, Arduino), SBCs (Raspberry Pi)
  • Motor drivers, servos, stepper systems, encoders
  • Pneumatic/hydraulic trainer kits; PLC panels

Software

  • CAD/CAE: SolidWorks, Fusion 360, ANSYS
  • Embedded/IDE: Keil, STM32CubeIDE, PlatformIO
  • Robotics/AI: MATLAB/Simulink, ROS, OpenCV, Python
  • Automation: TIA Portal, Studio 5000, LabVIEW

Practices

  • Rapid prototyping; PCB design and fabrication
  • Version control (Git); CI for firmware and simulation
  • System modeling, identification, and HIL testing
  • Safety, EMC, and compliance testing

Skills You Will Build

  • Mechanical design, tolerancing, and mechatronic integration
  • Sensor selection, interfacing, and signal conditioning
  • Embedded C/C++ and Python for control & perception
  • PID, state‑space control, and motion planning basics
  • Robot programming (ROS), robotic arm calibration
  • PLC programming (Ladder, FBD) and HMI/SCADA
  • Computer vision and edge AI deployment
  • Reliability engineering and systems safety
